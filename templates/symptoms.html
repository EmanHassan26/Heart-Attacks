Heart Attack Symptoms

Common Symptoms:

  • Chest pain or discomfort (pressure, squeezing, fullness)
  • Pain or discomfort in arms, back, neck, jaw, or stomach
  • Shortness of breath with or without chest discomfort
  • Cold sweat
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Lightheadedness or sudden dizziness
Note: Symptoms can vary between men and women. Women are more likely to experience shortness of breath, nausea/vomiting, and back or jaw pain.

First Aid Measures

If you suspect someone is having a heart attack:

  1. Call emergency services immediately (911 or your local emergency number)
  2. Help the person sit down and rest in a comfortable position
  3. Loosen any tight clothing
  4. Ask if they take chest pain medication (like nitroglycerin)
  5. If the person is unconscious, begin CPR if you're trained
  6. If available, use an AED (automated external defibrillator)
  7. Stay with the person until emergency responders arrive

What NOT to do:

  • Don't leave the person alone
  • Don't allow the person to deny symptoms and convince you not to call for help
  • Don't wait to see if symptoms go away
  • Don't give the person anything by mouth unless prescribed heart medication
